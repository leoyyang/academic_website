Invited by the **Jinan University Social Survey Center**, I delivered Lecture 55 in the
"Social Survey & Application" series on the policy shock created by Weibo's April 2022 IP
disclosure requirement. The project tracks 200 news accounts and the top comment chains
around each post to observe how geography-linked identity cues steer civic debate.

## Research overview

- **Data engineering.** I detailed the pipeline we built to capture pre/post policy posts,
  scrape reply trees, and annotate user locations while protecting user privacy.
- **Behavioral impact.** Comparing domestic and overseas commenters reveals divergent
  participation patterns once location tags become visible, alongside a measurable drop in
  hostile replies.
- **Implications for moderators.** Location transparency changes how online communities
  interpret credibility and allegiances, so we discussed how to pair machine flags with human
  review to keep deliberation open while curbing abuse.

We closed with a Q&A on fielding API-rate constraints, validating location cues, and extending
the design to other high-frequency social platforms.
